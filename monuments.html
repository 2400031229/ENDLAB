Historic Monuments of India

Explore the architectural marvels that define India's rich heritage

Taj Mahal

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this ivory-white marble mausoleum was built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Red Fort

A historic fortified palace of the Mughal emperors, serving as the main residence of the Mughal dynasty for nearly 200 years.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Qutub Minar

A 73-meter tall tapering tower of five stories, built in 1193 by Qutab-ud-din Aibak and later completed by his successor Iltutmish.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Hampi

The ruins of the Vijayanagara Empire, showcasing magnificent temples, royal complexes, and ancient market streets.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Ajanta & Ellora Caves

Ancient Buddhist, Hindu, and Jain cave temples carved into rock cliffs, dating from 2nd century BCE to 10th century CE.

Best Time to Visit: November to March

Charminar

A monument and mosque built in 1591, located in Hyderabad, Telangana. It has become a global icon of Hyderabad.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Gateway of India

An arch-monument built in the early 20th century in Mumbai, commemorating the landing of King George V and Queen Mary.

Best Time to Visit: November to February

Konark Sun Temple

A 13th-century CE Sun Temple at Konark about 35 kilometers northeast of Puri, designed as a gigantic chariot for the sun god Surya.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Mysore Palace

A historical palace and royal residence in Mysore, Karnataka. It is one of the most famous tourist attractions in India.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Hawa Mahal

The Palace of Winds in Jaipur, built in 1799 by Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh. Famous for its unique five-story exterior resembling a honeycomb.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Khajuraho Temples

A group of Hindu and Jain temples famous for their intricate sculptures and architectural brilliance, built between 950-1050 CE.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Sanchi Stupa

One of the oldest stone structures in India, built by Emperor Ashoka in the 3rd century BCE to house Buddhist relics.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Fatehpur Sikri

A fortified city built by Mughal Emperor Akbar, serving as the capital of the Mughal Empire from 1571 to 1585.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Meenakshi Temple

A historic Hindu temple located in Madurai, Tamil Nadu, dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareshwar.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Victoria Memorial

A large marble building in Kolkata, built between 1906 and 1921 to commemorate Queen Victoria's 25 years of rule in India.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Golconda Fort

A ruined city and fortress in Hyderabad, famous for its acoustic architecture and diamond mines that produced the famous Kohinoor diamond.

Best Time to Visit: October to February

Amber Fort

A majestic fort located in Amer, Rajasthan, built by Raja Man Singh I in 1592, known for its artistic Hindu style elements.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Brihadeeswara Temple

A Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, built by Raja Raja Chola I between 1003 and 1010 CE.

Best Time to Visit: November to February

Lotus Temple

A Baháʼí House of Worship in Delhi, notable for its flowerlike shape. Completed in 1986, it has become a prominent attraction in the city.

Best Time to Visit: October to March

Jaisalmer Fort

A massive sandstone fort in Jaisalmer, Rajasthan, built in 1156 AD by the Rajput ruler Rawal Jaisal. Known as the Golden Fort.

Best Time to Visit: October to March
